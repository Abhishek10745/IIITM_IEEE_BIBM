1st International Workshop on

AI-Driven Prediction for Biomedical Data Analysis

Using Image Processing, Graph Signal Processing and Computational Biology

Workshop Overview

The convergence of Image Processing, Graph Signal Processing (GSP), and Computational Biology represents a critical frontier in biomedical data analysis. As medical datasets expand across multi-modal dimensions—ranging from high-resolution histopathology scans to complex protein-protein interaction networks—there is a pressing need for robust artificial intelligence frameworks capable of synthesizing these distinct data topologies.

AIPBDA 2026 aims to convene leading researchers, clinical practitioners, and computational scientists to explore novel predictive models. The workshop focuses heavily on the integration of spatial imaging features with relational biological graphs to enhance diagnostic accuracy, disease prediction, and clinical decision support systems.

Call for Papers & Topics of Interest

We invite original, high-quality research submissions. Topics of interest include, but are not strictly limited to:

  • Deep Learning in Medical Imaging: Novel CNN and Vision Transformer (ViT) architectures for segmentation, classification, and anomaly detection in MRI, CT, and ultrasound data.
  • Graph Signal Processing & GNNs: Application of Graph Neural Networks for analyzing biological networks, multi-omics data, and molecular property prediction.
  • Explainable AI (XAI) in Biomedicine: Interpretability frameworks (e.g., SHAP, Integrated Gradients, Grad-CAM) applied to clinical AI systems to ensure transparency.
  • Multi-Modal Data Fusion: Methodologies for integrating spatial medical imaging with structural genomic/proteomic graphs.
  • Computational Biology Algorithms: Predictive modeling for genomics, transcriptomics, and pathway analysis.
